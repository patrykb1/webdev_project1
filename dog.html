German Shepherds

What is a German Shepherd?

German Shepherds are a German breed of dogs, intially bred for herding sheep

Today, German Shepherds are used in many roles, including disability assistance, search-and-rescue, police work, and warfare.

Appearance

German Shepherds are large dogs, covered in a characteristic black and brown coat. They have a strong build, with a long muzzle and erect ears.

Temperament

German Shepherds are known for their intelligence and loyalty. They are highly trainable and often excel in obedience training. They are also protective of their families, making them excellent guard dogs. However, if not trained properly, they can become antisocial and overprotective.
